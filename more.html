The River Nile

The Nile is a major north-flowing river in northeastern Africa. It flows into the Mediterranean Sea. The longest river in Africa, it has historically been considered the longest river in the world, though this has been contested by research suggesting that the Amazon River is slightly longer.

Amazon River

The Amazon River in South America is the largest river by discharge volume of water in the world, and the disputed longest river in the world in comparison to the Nile.

# Fun facts about rivers
1 A river is a large stream of freshwater. The beginning of a river is called the head. The end of the river is known as the mouth.
2 The current describes how fast and powerful the river is. Rivers that have a lot of water and a steep decline can be very fast.
3 Rivers change over time. Floods may make them larger, droughts cause them to shrink or dry up. People and animals change them too. People build dams, as do beavers
4 Rivers can carve out canyons in the landscape. The Grand Canyon in Arizona was carved by a roaring river.
5 The longest river in the world is the Nile in Africa. The Amazon River in South America carries the most water.
